redp makes it straightforward to go from an open Nova release to a structured, evidence-backed approval decision. Follow the steps below to generate your first guideline report and understand what the results mean.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the release in redp">
    Log in to [redp](https://redp.app) and select your Nova release from the **Releases** list. The release overview page shows the current deploy plan, component list, and any existing reports.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to the Nova Guideline tab">
    Inside the release, click the **Nova Guideline** tab. This tab lists all previously generated guideline reports for the release and lets you trigger a new one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure report scope">
    Click **New Report** and choose your scope:

    - **All components** — evaluate every component in the release deploy plan. This is the recommended default for a full release readiness check.
    - **Selected components** — pick a subset of components to evaluate. Use this for targeted re-evaluation after resolving a blocker on specific components.

    Confirm your selection and proceed to the next step.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Trigger report generation and poll for completion">
    Click **Generate Report**. redp begins collecting evidence from Jira and GitLab, evaluating readiness rules, and composing the AI-assisted summary. Report generation typically completes within a few minutes depending on the number of components in scope.

    The report status cycles through: `PENDING` → `RUNNING` → `COMPLETE` (or `FAILED`). Refresh the page or use the [API](/api/get-report) to poll for completion.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the approval decision and required actions">
    Once the report status is `COMPLETE`, open it to see:

    - The top-level **approval decision** (GO, CONDITIONAL\_GO, NO\_GO, or UNKNOWN)
    - A list of **blocking rules** that must be resolved before the release can proceed
    - A list of **conditional rules** that require acknowledgement or a filed waiver
    - The **AI-assisted release summary** with source-traced evidence references
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What to look for in the generated report

After the report completes, focus on these sections first:

- **Decision banner** — the coloured banner at the top of the report tells you immediately whether you have a GO, CONDITIONAL\_GO, or NO\_GO.
- **Rule evaluation table** — each readiness rule is listed with its outcome (PASS, CONDITIONAL, BLOCK) and the evidence that drove it.
- **Evidence coverage** — check the coverage percentage for each component. Low coverage may indicate missing Jira tickets or unlinked GitLab MRs.
- **Required actions** — any blocking or conditional rule surfaces a concrete action item. Assign each action to the relevant component owner before re-running the report.

<Note>
  The AI-assisted release summary is generated to help you communicate release context quickly, but it requires human review before use in a CMR or stakeholder communication. Always verify that the summary accurately reflects the evidence cited in the report.
</Note>

<Tip>
  If your report returns UNKNOWN, check the **Evidence Snapshot** section for any failed Jira or GitLab collection jobs, resolve the connectivity issue, and re-trigger the report.
</Tip>
